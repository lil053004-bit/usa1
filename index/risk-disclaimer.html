1. General Investment Risks

All investments carry risk. Before making any investment decision, you should carefully consider the following risks:

1.1 Market Risk

  • Stock prices can fluctuate widely based on market conditions, economic factors, and investor sentiment
  • Market volatility can result in rapid and substantial losses
  • Global events, political instability, and economic changes can negatively impact markets
  • Bear markets and corrections can result in significant portfolio declines

1.2 Company-Specific Risk

  • Individual companies may face financial difficulties, management issues, or operational challenges
  • Poor earnings reports or negative news can cause stock prices to decline sharply
  • Companies can go bankrupt, resulting in total loss of investment
  • Fraud, misconduct, or regulatory violations can devastate stock values

1.3 Liquidity Risk

  • Some stocks may be difficult to sell quickly at desired prices
  • Low trading volume can result in significant price slippage
  • Market disruptions can temporarily halt trading

1.4 Economic Risk

  • Inflation can erode the purchasing power of investment returns
  • Interest rate changes can negatively impact stock valuations
  • Economic recessions can cause widespread market declines
  • Currency fluctuations can affect international investments

  • AI models cannot predict unprecedented events or "black swan" scenarios
  • Machine learning algorithms can produce incorrect or misleading conclusions
  • AI cannot account for sudden market shocks, regulatory changes, or unforeseen events

2.2 Data Quality and Accuracy

  • AI analysis depends on the quality and accuracy of input data
  • Data errors, delays, or omissions can lead to incorrect analysis
